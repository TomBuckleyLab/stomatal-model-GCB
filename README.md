# stomatal-model-GCB

This repository contains code and data to generate figures in the paper, "A simple stomatal model that unifies the metabolic and hydraulic control of carbon and water flux," by Thomas N Buckley, Julien Lamour, David M Barnard, Daniel W Buckley, Andrew J Jarvis, Antonio Diaz-Espejo, Alistair Rogers and Lawren Sack, accepted for publication in Global Change Biology in June 2026.

The code to generate most figures draws on function definitions and initialization code in "main functions.R" and "initialization.R," respectively.

Some figures draw on previously published data. Of those files, the following are provided here:

 - antisense.csv: data from Fig 7B in von Caemmerer et al (2004, Journal of Experimental Botany 55:1157-1166)
 - celia almond.csv, celia grapevine.csv, celia olive.csv: data used in Rodriguez-Dominguez et al (2016, Plant Cell and Environment 39:2014-2026)
 - lamour aci.csv, lamour aq.csv, lamour gs.csv: data from Lamour et al (2022, Global Change Biology 28:3537-3556)
 - mills 2024 data.csv: data from Mills et al (2024, Plant Cell and Environment 47:3428-3446)

The following input files are also required (for Fig 3), and are accessible at repositories created by the original authors:

 - WTC4_data.csv: data originally published by Drake et al (2018, Global Change Biology 24:2390-2402) and later reanalyzed by Sicangco et al (2026, New Phytologist 250:1551-1568)
 - control_runs.Rdata, heatwave_runs.Rdata: these include predictions from the USO model for the Drake dataset above, generated by Sicangco et al (2026)
 - lin2015data.csv: data from Lin et al (2015, Nature Climate Change 5:459-464)
